## What the Conditions Have in Common

Look at every origin story above. A pattern emerges that is not anecdotal — it is structural.

```
CONDITION 1 — SOLITUDE OR SMALL GROUP:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Newton:       alone at Woolsthorpe during plague
Einstein:     alone at the patent office
Darwin:       alone on the Beagle, then alone at Down House
Mendel:       alone in the monastery garden
Faraday:      alone in the laboratory
Gödel:        individual proof
Wiles:        alone in a Cambridge attic for seven years

Not one was produced by a committee.
Not one emerged from a grant-funded team programme.
Not one appeared on an institutional roadmap.


CONDITION 2 — OUTSIDE OR AT THE EDGE OF THE INSTITUTION:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Einstein:     rejected for academic positions
Faraday:      no formal education, blacksmith's son
Mendel:       monastery, never received academic recognition
Avogadro:     ignored for 50 years
Darwin:       self-funded, deliberately outside the pressure
Ramanujan:    no formal training, India to Cambridge outsider
Turing:       worked on a problem nobody commissioned

The deeper inside the institution —
the less likely the fundamental law.
The further outside —
the more likely.
This is not selection bias.
This is the geometry of basins.


CONDITION 3 — NO INSTITUTIONAL COMMISSION:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Not one fundamental law was produced
in response to an institutional brief.

"We need a law describing motion" → nobody wrote Newton's laws.
"We need a theory of inheritance" → nobody wrote Mendel's laws.
"We need a unified field theory" → nobody wrote Maxwell's equations.

The institution cannot commission
what it cannot yet conceive of.
The new law defines new territory.
The institution does not know
the territory exists
until the individual finds it.


CONDITION 4 — IGNORED OR ATTACKED FIRST:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Copernicus:   published the year he died
Galileo:      tried by the Inquisition
Mendel:       ignored for 35 years, rediscovered after death
Avogadro:     ignored for 50 years
Boltzmann:    ridiculed by the establishment, died by suicide
              before his statistical mechanics was accepted
Darwin:       attacked by the church and by fellow scientists
Einstein:     dismissed early

The institution's first response to a fundamental law
is almost never acceptance.
It is rejection, ridicule, or silence.
Acceptance comes after.
Always after.
```
