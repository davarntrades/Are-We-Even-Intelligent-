## The Question

Every fundamental law in the history of science traces to one person.

Not a team. Not an institution. Not a committee.

One person.

This has happened so consistently — across 2,500 years, across every scientific domain, across every culture and civilisation that has produced science — that it cannot be coincidence.

It is a geometric inevitability.

This document explains exactly why.

  Fundamental laws are produced by outsiders.
  Always have been.
  Not because of talent.
  Not because of genius.
  Because of basin position.

  The outsider is geometrically positioned
  to do what the institution cannot.

  This is why it is always one person.
  This is why that person is always near the edge.

  Every time.
